Capacitance VideoDiscs

    Forgotten video format technology

    2 types of capacitance videodiscs:

    RCA's Capacitance Electronic Disc (CED)
    JVC's Video High Density (VHD)

Capacitance VideoDiscs

    Direct influence from vinyl record

About the tech: CED

    Record-style disc, 25cm diameter

    2 sided, 30 min of video on each side*

    Available from 1981-86 from RCA

About the tech: VHD

    Record-style disc, 25cm diameter

    2 sided, up to 60 min of video on each side

About the tech: VHD

    Demoed by JVC in 1978

    Launched in Japan later in 1983*

    North America left out

Background Info

    Printing Vinyl records was very cheap to do.

    Chose to practice cost cutting methods, instead of embracing new technologies and advancements.

    The only capacitance formats

Background Info

    Used the method of Capacitance to encode video and audio

    • “Capacitance can be defined as the ability of two adjacent conductors to store electric charge, and in the CED system these adjacent conductors are the carbon-loaded disc and the thin titanium electrode deposited on the diamond stylus.”

Using the technology

Discs kept in protective "caddys"

Insert caddy to player

Diamond stylus read the discs electronically

    Still in use today, popular as collectors items in Japan
